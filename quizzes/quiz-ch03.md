- C) Census data is unreliable for market research and should always be replaced with primary research
- D) Small business owners are too diverse in income levels to be monetized effectively
- E) The best early customers are always enterprise-size companies, not SMBs

<summary>Show Answer & Explanations</summary>

## Question 4

- A) $10B markets are too large for small teams to address effectively
- B) It starts with a top-down TAM estimate and then applies an arbitrary 10% capture rate with no explanation of how that market share will actually be earned
- C) Investors prefer markets under $1B because they have less competition
- D) The 10% capture rate underestimates how much market share a startup can realistically win
- E) TAM calculations are legally prohibited from being used in investor pitch materials

<details>
<summary>Show Answer & Explanations</summary>

- E) Offering one pricing tier rather than multiple tiers to avoid confusing prospects

<details>
<summary>Show Answer & Explanations</summary>

- A) ❌ Geographic focus is one possible form of beachhead, but the chapter's "one segment deep" principle is about customer type definition, not geography alone.

Spreading across multiple segments simultaneously produces a product optimized for no one — as illustrated by Toast's counterfactual.
- C) ❌ Feature depth is a product development concept; the beachhead strategy is a go-to-market and customer segmentation strategy, not a product scope decision.
- D) ❌ Hiring a single salesperson is a resource constraint decision, not the strategic logic of the beachhead.
- E) ❌ Pricing tier simplicity may support a beachhead but is not the definition of "one segment deep."
