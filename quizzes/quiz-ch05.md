## Question 1

The chapter opens with the quote: *"You're not selling software. You're selling transformation."* What does this mean for how a pitch should be structured?

- A) Pitches should avoid any technical product demonstrations and focus only on revenue projections
- B) The pitch must frame the product not as a feature set but as a before-and-after story — showing the specific transformation in the customer's situation, not the tool that enables it
- C) Software products are inferior to service businesses and should be reframed as transformation consulting
- D) The word "software" should never appear in investor pitch decks
- E) Founders should promise transformational outcomes even when the product is still a concept

<details>
<summary>Show Answer & Explanations</summary>

- A) ❌ The chapter includes a live demo as a critical component of the pitch (Section 5.5) — avoiding product demonstrations would undermine credibility, not enhance it.
- ⭐ B) ✅ "You're selling transformation" means investors and customers need to see the before-and-after — what the customer's world looks like without you, and what it becomes with you. The product is the mechanism; the transformation is the value. Pitches that lead with features (software capabilities) instead of transformation (outcomes for the customer) fail to connect emotionally or strategically.
- C) ❌ The chapter is not making a hierarchy claim between software and services — the quote is about framing strategy within the pitch, applicable to all product types.
- D) ❌ There is no prohibition on the word "software" — the advice is about what to foreground (transformation) rather than what words to avoid.
- E) ❌ Promising outcomes without evidence is the opposite of credibility. The chapter emphasizes that "data without story is a spreadsheet; story without data is a pitch from someone who hasn't done the work."

- C) The 10-slide deck is only appropriate for seed-stage companies; Series B decks require 30+ slides

- E) Objections should always be redirected to the co-founder to demonstrate team depth

<details>
<summary>Show Answer & Explanations</summary>
